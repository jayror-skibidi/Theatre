Discover the Art of Storytelling, Ghibli Style.


Step into the magic of Studio Ghibli! At Studio Ghibli Theatre, we bring the enchanting worlds of Ghibli films to life with exclusive insights, unforgettable stories, and endless imagination. Your journey into wonder starts here.



Ghibli Films


Howl's Moving Castle

A magical tale of a young woman cursed into old age and her enchanting journey with a mysterious wizard named Howl, set in a world where magic and war collide.

Ponyo

A heartwarming story about a goldfish who longs to become human, and her friendship with a young boy named Sosuke, set against the backdrop of an enchanting underwater world.

My Neighbour Totoro

A delightful film about two sisters who discover magical forest creatures, including the iconic Totoro, as they navigate life in the countryside while their mother is ill.

Spirited Away

A spellbinding adventure of a young girl named Chihiro, who stumbles into a spirit world and must work in a bathhouse to free herself and her parents from a mysterious curse.

Grave of the Fireflies

A powerful and emotional story about two siblings struggling to survive in Japan during the aftermath of World War II, highlighting the human cost of war.

Kiki's Delivery Service

The story of a young witch, Kiki, who sets out on her own for a year of independence, learning about life, responsibility, and friendship in a charming seaside town.

The Cat Returns

A whimsical and imaginative adventure where a young girl is drawn into a fantastical world of talking cats and must find her way home with the help of a mysterious Baron.

Arrietty

Based on The Borrowers, this enchanting film follows a tiny girl, Arrietty, who lives secretly with her family beneath the floorboards of a house, and her friendship with a human boy.


Ghibli Authors

Hayao Miyazaki

A legendary filmmaker, director, and co-founder of Studio Ghibli, Miyazaki is known for his imaginative storytelling, richly detailed worlds, and complex characters. His works often explore themes of nature, flight, pacifism, and human resilience, making him one of the most influential animators in the world. Some of his masterpieces include Spirited Away, My Neighbor Totoro, and Howl's Moving Castle.

Isao Takahata

A co-founder of Studio Ghibli and director, Takahata was known for his emotionally impactful and visually distinct films. Unlike Miyazaki’s focus on fantasy, Takahata’s works, such as Grave of the Fireflies and Only Yesterday, often dealt with realistic settings and deep human emotions. His unique style and storytelling approach made him a vital part of the studio’s artistic diversity.

Toshio Suzuki

A producer and co-founder of Studio Ghibli, Suzuki has been a key figure in the success of the studio. As a longtime collaborator with Miyazaki and Takahata, he has helped bring Ghibli’s films to life. His keen insight into storytelling and production has made him an essential part of Ghibli's legacy, overseeing many of its iconic films, including Kiki's Delivery Service and Ponyo.


This site is not affiliated with Studio Ghibli but celebrates its artistry and influence on animation and storytelling.
